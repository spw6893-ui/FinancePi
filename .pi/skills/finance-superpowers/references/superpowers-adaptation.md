# Superpowers Adaptation for FinancePi

This skill is pruned from the local `obra/Superpowers` clone into a finance research methodology.

## Retained principles

- Use skills before ad-hoc answering when a domain workflow applies.
- Understand context before producing a plan or conclusion.
- Ask targeted questions only when they change the answer.
- Explore alternative approaches before locking into one path.
- Keep plans executable: define what evidence, data, calculations, and user decisions are needed.
- Verify conclusions against data and disconfirming evidence.

## Pruned software-engineering parts

- Do not require TDD, commits, branches, or worktrees for finance analysis.
- Do not force an implementation-plan document unless the user asks for a research plan artifact.
- Do not use code-review or subagent-review language for investment work.
- Do not make the user approve every section of a report; ask only when a decision fork matters.

## Finance replacement

The finance equivalent of "brainstorm before implementation" is:

- identify the investment decision,
- determine what would change the user's action,
- choose the right modeling lens,
- gather or compute decisive evidence,
- challenge the thesis with disconfirming data,
- leave a reusable research artifact when the work is substantial.

The finance equivalent of "verification before completion" is:

- cite source/asOf/latestAt/filed dates,
- flag unavailable or stale data,
- distinguish tool output from interpretation,
- show which assumptions drive the conclusion,
- state what would falsify the view.
