# Equity Research Workflows

Use for public equity coverage, earnings work, thesis tracking, catalyst calendars, and idea generation.

## Earnings analysis

1. Pull reported results, filing facts, and recent news.
2. Compare against prior period and expectations only when sourced estimates are available.
3. Identify revenue, margin, EPS/net income, segment, KPI, and guidance deltas.
4. Separate reported facts from management commentary and your interpretation.
5. End with what changed in the thesis, key risks, and follow-up checks.

## Earnings preview

1. Identify reporting date, consensus expectations, key KPIs, and management guide when available.
2. Build bull/base/bear signposts around the metrics that matter for the business.
3. List likely catalysts and debate points.
4. Avoid inventing consensus or guide if no source is available.

## Initiating coverage / single-name deep dive

1. Business model and segment structure.
2. Industry context and peers.
3. Historical financials and key KPIs.
4. Valuation framework.
5. Thesis, catalysts, risks, and what would change the view.

## Thesis tracker

For an existing thesis:

- record the thesis claim,
- list required evidence,
- track confirming and disconfirming events,
- update confidence only after source-backed changes.

## Catalyst calendar

Track events such as:

- earnings dates,
- investor days,
- product launches,
- regulatory events,
- macro/commodity data releases,
- lockups or corporate actions.

Each catalyst should include date, source, affected names, expected metric/signpost, and uncertainty.

## Idea generation

Start with the user's direction:

- long, short, or both
- value, growth, quality, event-driven, special situation
- sector/geography/market cap
- theme

Then screen and produce candidates with thesis hook, catalyst, risk, and next work. Do not present screens as conclusions.
